About Project

Built with Purpose

This project, officially titled "Solar Assisted Electric Multipurpose Agricultural Vehicle", is developed as a final year diploma project by Mechanical Engineering students of Dibrugarh Polytechnic, Assam.

We named our system KRIXHI MATE — a simple and recognizable identity representing a practical, eco-friendly solution designed to support modern agricultural activities using solar-assisted electric power.

What It Does

Our Main Features

Three core capabilities that make farming smarter and easier.

01 / 03
📡

Wireless Control

Control the vehicle wirelessly using our smart mobile app with real-time feedback. No manual intervention needed — operate from a distance with full precision.

02 / 03
🌱

Seed Sowing

Automatic seed dispensing mechanism for precise and efficient farming. Uniform seed distribution at optimal intervals with zero manual effort.

03 / 03
💧

Water / Pesticide Spraying

Efficient spraying system for water, pesticide, and fertilizer with uniform coverage. Reduces waste and ensures every inch of the field is treated.

How We Built It

Fabrication Journey

Every step from concept to reality — our complete build timeline.

JAN 2026
Planning Phase
Concept & Design

Initial brainstorming, problem identification, and concept design. We researched existing agricultural vehicles and defined our core objectives: solar power, wireless control, and multipurpose farming tools.

FEB 2026
Design Phase
CAD Modelling & Material Procurement

Created detailed technical drawings and CAD models. Selected materials, sourced components — chassis steel, motors, solar panels, microcontrollers — and began procurement.

MAR 2026
Fabrication Phase
Chassis & Frame Construction

Cutting, welding, and assembling the main chassis. Mounting the motor, wheels, solar panel frame, and protective structure. This was the most hands-on and labor-intensive phase of the project.

APR 2026
Integration Phase
Electronics & App Integration

Wiring the electrical system, integrating the solar charging circuit, programming the wireless control module, and developing the companion mobile app for real-time control and monitoring.

MAY 2026
Testing Phase
Field Testing & Final Submission

Conducted field trials, tested all three core functions — wireless control, seed sowing, and spraying — and fine-tuned the system. Prepared final documentation and project report for submission.
